# Community Guidelines and Code of Conduct

## Overview

The Crazy Gary project is built on collaboration, respect, and inclusivity. This document establishes our community guidelines and code of conduct to ensure a welcoming and productive environment for all participants.

## Code of Conduct

### Our Commitment

We are dedicated to providing a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

### Standards

#### Expected Behavior

We expect all community members to:

- **Be Respectful**: Treat all community members with respect and kindness
- **Be Inclusive**: Welcome and support people of all backgrounds and identities
- **Be Collaborative**: Work together constructively and supportively
- **Be Patient**: Help newcomers learn and grow
- **Be Honest**: Communicate openly and transparently
- **Be Professional**: Maintain professional standards in all interactions
- **Be Constructive**: Provide feedback that helps improve the project
- **Be Mindful**: Consider the impact of your words and actions on others

#### Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered harassment and are unacceptable:

- **Harassment**: Offensive verbal comments, deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following
- **Discrimination**: Harassment based on any protected characteristic
- **Personal Attacks**: Violent threats or language directed against another person
- **Sexual Harassment**: Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- **Trolling**: Deliberate attempts to provoke or derail discussions
- **Personal Disruptions**: Repeated interruptions or derailing of discussions
- **Private Harassment**: Publishing others' private information without permission
- **Retaliation**: Punishing community members for reporting harassment

### Protected Characteristics

We do not tolerate harassment or discrimination based on:

- Age
- Body size
- Disability
- Ethnicity
- Sex characteristics
- Gender identity and expression
- Level of experience
- Education
- Socio-economic status
- Nationality
- Personal appearance
- Race
- Religion
- Sexual identity and orientation

## Conflict Resolution

### Escalation Process

#### Level 1: Direct Resolution
1. **Address Directly**: If comfortable, address the issue directly with the person
2. **Private Communication**: Use private messages to discuss sensitive matters
3. **Cooling Off**: Take a break if emotions are running high

#### Level 2: Moderator Intervention
1. **Report to Moderators**: Contact moderators through appropriate channels
2. **Document the Issue**: Provide clear documentation of the problem
3. **Mediation**: Allow moderators to facilitate discussion

#### Level 3: Maintainer Involvement
1. **Escalate to Maintainers**: For serious or unresolved issues
2. **Formal Investigation**: Maintainers will investigate thoroughly
3. **Decision and Action**: Appropriate action will be taken

### Reporting Process

#### How to Report
```markdown
## Reporting Harassment or Code of Conduct Violations

### What to Include
- Description of the incident
- When and where it occurred
- Who was involved
- Any witnesses
- Screenshots or evidence (if applicable)
- Your preferred resolution

### How to Report
- **GitHub Issues**: Use the "Report Issue" template
- **Email**: safety@project.com (for private issues)
- **Discord**: Contact @moderators or use the #report channel

### What Happens Next
1. **Acknowledgment**: You'll receive confirmation within 24 hours
2. **Investigation**: We'll investigate the incident thoroughly
3. **Resolution**: Appropriate action will be taken
4. **Follow-up**: We'll check in to ensure the issue is resolved
```

#### Investigation Process
1. **Initial Review**: Moderators review the report within 24 hours
2. **Evidence Gathering**: Collect relevant information and testimony
3. **Parties Involved**: All parties may be contacted for input
4. **Decision Making**: Maintainers make final decision on action
5. **Implementation**: Appropriate consequences are applied
6. **Follow-up**: Monitor situation to ensure resolution

### Consequences

#### Minor Violations
- **Warning**: Private, written warning with clarification of expectations
- **Temporary Suspension**: Temporary suspension from any interaction
- **Public Apology**: Request for public apology

#### Serious Violations
- **Permanent Ban**: Permanent ban from any interaction with community
- **Account Suspension**: Suspension of GitHub, Discord, and other accounts
- **Content Removal**: Removal of harassing content

#### Appeal Process
- **Right to Appeal**: All individuals have the right to appeal decisions
- **Appeal Window**: Appeals must be submitted within 30 days
- **Review Process**: Independent review by different maintainers
- **Final Decision**: Appeals decisions are final

## Inclusive Practices

#### Communication Accessibility

#### Language Considerations
- Use simple, clear English

- Provide translations when possible
- Use visual examples when words are unclear

#### Time Zones and Scheduling
